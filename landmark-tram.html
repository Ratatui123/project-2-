Roosevelt Island Tramway

An aerial cable car offering stunning skyline views over the East River.

Connecting Manhattan’s Upper East Side with Roosevelt Island, the tramway is a unique piece of New York’s transit system and a one-of-a-kind commuter experience.

Featured Landmark

The Roosevelt Island Tramway — a rare aerial commuter line — glides high above the East River connecting Roosevelt Island and Manhattan.

Opened in 1976, the tram was the first commuter aerial tramway in the United States. Spanning about 3,140 feet, it carries passengers above the East River with a four-minute ride that offers sweeping skyline views.

Today, it operates as part of the MTA system, accepting both MetroCards and OMNY, and continues to serve commuters and tourists alike from the Manhattan terminal at 59th Street & Second Avenue.
