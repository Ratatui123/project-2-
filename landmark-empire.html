Empire State Building

An Art Deco masterpiece that once held the title of the world’s tallest building.

Standing proudly in Midtown Manhattan, the Empire State Building remains one of the most recognized and celebrated landmarks in the world.

Featured Landmark

The Empire State Building is an Art Deco skyscraper in Midtown Manhattan that once held the title of the world’s tallest building.

Built: Constructed in a record 1 year and 45 days from 1930–1931, the 102-story tower was designed by the firm Shreve, Lamb & Harmon Associates and opened on May 1, 1931, when President Hoover pressed a button in Washington, D.C. to light it up.

Today: Its rich history, timeless silhouette, and observation decks make it a global tourist destination and an official New York City and National Historic Landmark.
