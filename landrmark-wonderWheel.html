Wonder Wheel

A century-old Ferris wheel that still lights up the Coney Island skyline.

Rising above the boardwalk since 1920, the Wonder Wheel is one of New York City’s most beloved seaside icons, offering sweeping views of the Atlantic and the vibrant energy of Coney Island.

Featured Landmark

Wonder Wheel in Coney Island — a historic landmark overlooking the boardwalk and Atlantic Ocean.

Built: The Wonder Wheel was constructed in 1920 by the Eccentric Ferris Wheel Company and has operated continuously ever since. It became a New York City Landmark in 1989 and is still family-run by the Vourderis family as part of Deno’s Wonder Wheel Amusement Park.

Today: It remains a centerpiece of Deno’s Wonder Wheel Amusement Park, open seasonally from spring through fall. It features 24 passenger cars — some fixed, some sliding — with views of the beach, ocean, and surrounding amusement district.
