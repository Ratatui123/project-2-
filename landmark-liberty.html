Statue of Liberty

An enduring symbol of freedom and friendship between nations.

Standing proudly in New York Harbor, the Statue of Liberty represents hope, liberty, and the lasting bond between France and the United States.

Featured Landmark

The Statue of Liberty — designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and engineered by Gustave Eiffel — remains one of the most recognizable symbols in the world.

Built in France and dedicated in 1886, the statue was a gift from the French people to the United States. Shipped in pieces and reassembled on Liberty Island, it stands as a monument to democracy and friendship between nations.

Over the decades, the statue has welcomed millions arriving in New York Harbor. It remains both a national monument and a global icon of freedom.
