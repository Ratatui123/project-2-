Staten Island Ferry

Free rides, skyline views, and a piece of New York history.

The Staten Island Ferry links Manhattan and Staten Island across New York Harbor, offering one of the city’s most scenic commutes — and it’s completely free.

Featured Landmark

The Staten Island Ferry offers one of the best free views of New York Harbor — from the Statue of Liberty to Lower Manhattan’s skyline.

The ferry service began in 1817 with the Richmond Turnpike Company and later came under New York City operation in 1905. It now carries millions of passengers each year between Staten Island and Lower Manhattan.

Beyond transportation, the ferry has become a local favorite for sightseeing — offering breathtaking views of the harbor, Ellis Island, and the Statue of Liberty.
